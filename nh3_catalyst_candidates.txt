# NH3 FORMATION CATALYST CANDIDATES
# 20 promising candidates for N2 + H2 → NH3 catalysis

1. [Fe(N₂)(CO)₄]⁻ - Homogeneous (anion)
   Strong back‑bonding to N₂, labile CO ligands for H₂ uptake

2. [Ru(N₂)(H)₂(PNP)] - Homogeneous (pincer)
   Pincer scaffold enforces vacant site for N₂; two hydrides pre‑loaded

3. [Mo(N₂)(CO)₅]⁻ - Homogeneous (anion)
   High dπ → π* N₂ back‑donation, multiple CO ligands

4. [W(N₂)(PMe₃)₄]⁺ - Homogeneous (cation)
   Strong N₂ binding, labile phosphine ligands for H₂ activation

5. [Ir(POCOP)(H)₂] - Homogeneous (Ir‑pincer)
   Two trans‑hydrides ready for H‑transfer, vacant site for N₂

6. [Co(bis‑diazabutadiene)(CO)₂] - Homogeneous (Co‑complex)
   Redox‑active ligand can store electrons, CO provides labile sites

7. [Ni(P₂N₂)(N₂)] - Homogeneous (Ni‑pincer)
   Low‑spin Ni⁰ binds N₂ side‑on; pincer framework stabilises redox states

8. [Re(bpy)(N₂)(CO)₃]⁺ - Homogeneous (Re‑complex)
   Strong π‑acceptor CO ligands, redox‑active bipyridine

9. [Fe₂(μ‑S)₂(CO)₆] - Homogeneous (cluster)
   Fe–Fe bond provides electron reservoir; sulfide bridge mimics nitrogenase

10. [MoFe₃S₄(CO)₉]⁻ - Homogeneous (cluster)
    Cubane core supplies multi‑electron reduction

11. Fe(111) surface - Heterogeneous (single‑atom)
    Isolated Fe atom, graphene facilitates H₂ spill‑over

12. Ru(0001) surface - Heterogeneous (metal surface)
    Classical Haber‑Bosch catalyst

13. Co‑doped MoS₂ - Heterogeneous (2‑D chalcogenide)
    S‑vacancies for N₂, Co introduces d‑states for H₂

14. Fe‑N‑C - Heterogeneous (single‑site)
    Fe‑N₄ mimics nitrogenase active site

15. Supported Ru‑pincer on silica - Heterogeneous (organometallic)
    Molecular pincer + high surface area

16. [Os(N₂)(H)₂(PMe₃)₄] - Homogeneous (Os‑complex)
    Strong N₂ back‑bonding, two hydrides pre‑installed

17. [Ti(η⁶‑C₆H₆)(N₂)(H)₂] - Homogeneous (Ti⁰)
    Benzene stabilises low‑valent Ti, N₂ side‑on binding

18. [V(N₂)(CO)₄]⁻ - Homogeneous (anion)
    High d‑electron count for strong N₂ back‑bonding

19. [Mn(bpy)(N₂)(CO)₃]⁺ - Homogeneous (Mn‑complex)
    Cheap first‑row metal, bipyridine mediates electron flow

20. Pd‑nanoclusters on TiO₂ - Heterogeneous (photo‑thermal)
    Pd dissociates H₂, TiO₂ assists N₂ activation under light
